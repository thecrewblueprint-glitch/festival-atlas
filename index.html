Festival Production Labor Map

A Pages-ready research build focused only on staging labor and production-related festival work: staging, rigging, lighting, audio, video/LED, power, site ops, logistics, scenic, backline, stage management, and production office pathways.

Research rule: use this as a lead map, not a guarantee. Verify current vendors, IATSE jurisdiction, classification, pay terms, safety requirements, and production contacts before outreach.

Research workflow

Questions for every festival

  • Who builds the stage?
  • Who supplies rigging?
  • Who supplies lighting?
  • Who supplies audio?
  • Who supplies video/LED?
  • Who handles site ops?
  • Who handles power?
  • Which IATSE local applies?
  • Which nonunion labor companies staff the event?
  • Who is the production manager or production company?

Minimum useful record

  • Festival name, city, state, month/window, producer/promoter.
  • Production departments active at the event.
  • Known or likely employer channels by department.
  • Union/local jurisdiction notes.
  • Nonunion labor company leads.
  • Source URL and last verification date.
  • Status: confirmed, needs verification, lead only, historical, or user supplied.

Worker outreach packet

  • One-page resume by department.
  • Short photo portfolio with captions.
  • Credential wallet and PPE/tool list.
  • Availability calendar and travel radius.
  • Call history, rates, and references.

Classification guardrail

Never assume a festival job is 1099. Track the actual model: W-2, union referral, temp staffing, direct hire, subcontractor, volunteer, or unknown. The app should surface uncertainty rather than hide it.
