Worker roadmap

A practical sequence for turning research into booked work without wasting effort.

Step 1 — Build your worker packet

  • One-page stagehand resume with departments: staging, lighting, audio, video, rigging support, site ops.
  • Short portfolio: 6–10 photos with captions showing scale, role, location, and year.
  • Credential wallet: OSHA card if applicable, lift/fork certificates if applicable, first aid, rigging training, driver/license if relevant.
  • Travel profile: home city, available regions, vehicle status, lodging flexibility, blackout dates.

Step 2 — Target the right channel

  • Union-heavy market: contact the relevant IATSE local and ask about referral/list process.
  • Festival-heavy market: identify labor vendors, staging/audio/lighting/video vendors, and site operations contractors.
  • Corporate/arena-heavy market: apply to labor companies and venue crew pools.
  • Touring-vendor path: focus on specialty proof, reliability, travel readiness, and referrals.

Step 3 — Track outreach

  • Log date contacted, contact method, response, required documents, and next follow-up date.
  • Save rate terms, minimum call, overtime rules, travel/lodging terms, cancellation policy.
  • Tag each opportunity by engagement type so tax/admin expectations are clear.

Step 4 — Protect yourself

  • Do not assume every festival call is 1099. Confirm classification and paperwork.
  • Do not perform rigging, lift, electrical, or specialized work without authorization and training.
  • Keep invoices, call sheets, contracts, mileage, lodging, receipts, and payment records organized.
  • Prioritize reputable companies with clear terms and safe work practices.

About this tool

What it is

A single-file, dependency-free web app for researching U.S. festival-production work opportunities. It is designed for stagehands and production technicians who need a visual, searchable interface instead of scattered notes.

It is intentionally static and portable: no backend, no accounts, no external libraries.

What would make it better

  • Verified source links for every vendor/festival relationship.
  • Saved notes and outreach tracking using local storage.
  • Calendar reminders for staffing windows.
  • Exportable CSV of target companies.
  • Worker profile and credential checklist.
  • Separate data files once a backend or repo workflow exists.

Classification warning

Stagehand work may be 1099, W-2, union referral, temp agency payroll, or direct employment. The app uses “freelance stagehand” as a worker-search term, not a legal classification. Verify paperwork and tax obligations for each job.

Safety warning

This app does not certify a worker for rigging, lift operation, electrical work, fall protection, or any specialized task. Follow employer procedures, local jurisdiction, qualified-person requirements, and applicable safety rules.
