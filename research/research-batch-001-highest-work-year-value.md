# Research Batch 001 — Highest Work-Year Value Targets

Date: 2026-06-21  
Branch: research-version  
Targets: Breakaway, Country Thunder, Summerfest, Electric Forest, New Orleans Jazz & Heritage Festival

---

## Scoring Method

This batch scores opportunity value based on work-year usefulness:

- lodging / housing signal
- paid travel / mileage / flight signal
- per diem signal
- work-window length
- hiring route clarity
- repeat or multi-market value
- source confidence

The score does not measure event popularity. It measures whether the target is likely to help fill open time in the work year.

---

## Batch Findings

### 1. Breakaway Music Festival

Recommended score: **74**

Reasoning:

- Multi-market run from April through November.
- Official site lists 2026 dates across Dallas, Tampa, Arizona, Atlanta, Ohio, Las Vegas, Minnesota, New York City, Michigan, Massachusetts, Philadelphia, Carolina, Utah, and Houston.
- Official site advertises travel packages that include ticket and hotel-stay components, but this is an attendee signal, not confirmed worker lodging.
- Hiring route remains unverified; likely needs city-by-city vendor/labor research.

Next human action:

Split Breakaway into city-specific records and verify whether Breakaway Group, local venues, staging/audio/lighting vendors, or local labor providers staff each market.

### 2. Country Thunder

Recommended score: **66**

Reasoning:

- Confirmed active U.S. markets include Florida and Wisconsin.
- Florida is confirmed May 8–10, 2026 at Coachman Park, Clearwater.
- Wisconsin is confirmed July 16–19, 2026 and has public tickets/camping/hotel-package signals.
- Multi-market pattern gives it work-year value, but the current hiring/labor route is not publicly clear.

Next human action:

Split Country Thunder into market-specific records and verify local labor providers, production vendors, camping/hotel relevance, and any travel-crew path.

### 3. Summerfest

Recommended score: **76**

Reasoning:

- Strongest Batch 1 public hiring-route signal because Summerfest links directly to a jobs page.
- Official site describes 9 days, 600 artists, and Milwaukee location.
- The footer links to jobs, accommodations, Milwaukee World Festival, Summerfest Tech, American Family Insurance Amphitheater, and BMO Pavilion.
- Long run and permanent venue ecosystem make this a strong work-year target.

Next human action:

Use the official jobs route to check seasonal/operations/production-adjacent roles; separately verify stagehand/vendor labor path for Henry Maier Festival Park and Summerfest build/strike windows.

---

### 4. Electric Forest

Recommended score: **62**

Reasoning:

- Official site confirms June 25–28, 2026.
- Official navigation includes Plug In Programs and camping/shuttle-related add-ons.
- Camping/shuttle info is a public festival signal, not confirmed worker lodging or worker travel.
- Work window is short on public event dates, but build/strike likely extends the real work window; this needs human verification.

Next human action:

Verify whether Plug In Programs are volunteer-only or can lead to paid work; identify actual staging, rigging, lighting, audio, site ops, and Michigan labor providers.

---

### 5. New Orleans Jazz & Heritage Festival

Recommended score: **60**

Reasoning:

- 2026 dates are publicly reported as April 23 through May 3.
- Public reporting states crews had begun building on the Fair Grounds field before the festival.
- Long two-weekend format and major local economic impact make it valuable, but the production/labor hiring route is not yet publicly clear.
- Lodging, travel, and per diem remain unknown publicly.

Next human action:

Identify the New Orleans production vendor/labor route and whether paid travel, per diem, or lodging exists; prioritize local crew contacts and public vendor credits.
