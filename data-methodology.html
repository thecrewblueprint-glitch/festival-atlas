Production Atlas

How the Data Works

Production Atlas organizes public information into planning views for live-event production work research.

Data model

Each opportunity record can include a festival name, city, state, venue or site, public event dates, production departments, public producer or promoter information, public source references, and planning notes that are safe for public display.

Opportunities

Opportunity records identify events that may create live-event production work. These records are used by the Opportunities, Calendar, Map, and Schedule pages.

Departments

Departments group the work by production area, such as staging, rigging, lighting, audio, video, power, site operations, logistics, scenic, backline, stage management, and production office.

Employers

Employer records point to official public company pages, careers pages, application pages, contact pages, or relevant organization pages. General employer routes are not treated as confirmed event-specific contracts.

Sources

Source references are kept on the Sources page. This keeps event popups and employer views focused on work mapping while still allowing public references to be reviewed separately.
